Are young people meeting the recommended active days?
The data shows a striking contrast, no student reached even 15% of the recommended total active days. The most active user recorded just 6 active days, while several users logged only 1 day each. This demonstrates a significant shortfall in consistent physical activity, reinforcing the concern that most students are not meeting the basic daily movement requirements. The data highlights the need for increased awareness, motivation, and opportunities to help young people integrate more activity into their daily lives.
  • No student reached even 15% of the recommended active days.
  • The highest was 6 active days out of a possible 45.
  • Many students had only 1 or 2 active days.
Figure 3: Intensity of physical activity in the last 1.5 months among users
Are young people engaging in physical activity at the recommended intensity?
While students are engaging in physical activity, most are not meeting the recommended intensity levels. Only a few users reach close to the suggested amount of vigorous activity (3 days/week). The majority rely on light or moderate intensity, highlighting a need for more high-effort movement to meet health guidelines. According to physical activity guidelines for adolescents, young people should engage in at least 60 minutes of moderate to vigorous physical activity daily, with vigorous intensity recommended on at least 3 days per week.
  • Vigorous activity is rare among students.
  • Most activity is light or moderate intensity.
  • Few users approach the guideline of 3 days/week vigorous activity.
Figure 4: Active to Sedentary ratios in the last 1.5 months among users
Are young people balancing their active and sedentary time effectively?
The Active/Sedentary Ratios chart reveals that most students spend significantly more time being sedentary than active, with no ratio exceeding 0.25. This means that even the most active student is sedentary for four times longer than they are active. According to national guidelines, young people should be active for at least 60 minutes daily and limit screen time to 2 hours. These low ratios indicate that the majority of students are not meeting these recommendations, highlighting a broader issue of insufficient physical activity in youth.
  • No student had an active/sedentary ratio above 0.25.
  • Students are sedentary at least 4 times longer than active.
  • Most are not meeting national recommendations for activity vs. screen time.

Final Summary

The collected data highlights a clear and concerning trend: most young people are not meeting recommended physical activity guidelines and are spending excessive time being sedentary, especially on screens. Very few students achieve the daily or weekly targets for activity duration, intensity, or active days. Screen time consistently exceeds healthy limits, and sedentary behaviour dominates daily routines. These findings reinforce the urgent need for increased awareness, targeted interventions, and supportive environments to help young people move more, sit less, and develop lifelong healthy habits.
